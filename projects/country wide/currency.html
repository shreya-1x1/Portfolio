Important Guidelines for Currency Exchange in Bangalore.

Eligibility

Only resident Indians are allowed to purchase foreign currency to travel abroad.

Currency Limits

You can carry up to USD 3000 equivalent in cash and load up to USD 247000 equivalent on a forex card per traveler.

Currency distribution

It’s recommended to carry 30% of your foreign currency needs in cash and 70% on a Forex card for convenience and safety






Payment Methods

Cash payments for foreign currency are capped at INR 49,999. For amounts above this, a bank transfer is required.

Source Funds

Forex payments must originate from the traveler’s savings account, not from any third party, current account, NRE, or NRO accounts.

Taxes

All forex transactions are subject to GST and TCS, where applicable. Use our GST Calculator & TCS Calculator to determine the exact amounts.

Verification

Always check the amount and condition of the currency before leaving the counter or upon delivery.

Timing

Due to potential availability issues, it is advisable to purchase foreign currency at least 2 weeks before your travel date.

Currency Conversion

Any unspent foreign currency or remaining balance on forex cards can be converted back into INR after your trip at the current exchange rates.

    As per RBI guidelines, Indian residents can carry foreign currency up to USD 3,000 for personal travel. Higher amounts need to be declared and require valid documentation.

    Yes, a PAN card is mandatory for transactions exceeding ₹25,000. For lower amounts, alternate ID proof can be used.

    You can carry up to ₹25,000 in Indian currency while traveling internationally. For foreign currency, the limit depends on the purpose of travel and destination.


    How much foreign currency can I carry into India?

    You can bring unlimited foreign currency into India, but amounts exceeding USD 5,000 (cash) or USD 10,000 (cash + traveler’s cheques) must be declared at customs.
