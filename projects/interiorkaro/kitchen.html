Kitchen Layouts...

Every space is best utilised if configured as per the Door, Window, Chimney, Duct placement, etc.


One of the most followed design philosophies is "The Kitchen Working Triangle"which determines the three main work areas: the Sink, the Stove and the Refrigerator to be laid out to loosely form a triangle, enabling the user to perform day-to-day tasks with relative ease and without obstruction




Straight Kitchen

A minimalistic approach to kitchen design with all the units aligned to a single wall. It is space-saving and is suitable for smaller homes/ studio apartments.

Parallel Kitchen

With the storage and counter placed along the two walls, this design provides ample space and efficient workflow.

L Shaped Kitchen

One of the most preferred layout, it utilizes two adjacent walls and gives versatility to the space. Best suitable for medium-sized apartments.




U Shaped Kitchen

This three-sided wall design layout provides ample work and storage space which can be adapted in spacious homes.

Island Kitchen

A layout that defines luxury with the provision of extra worktops, is considered to be the most flexible and convenient design.

G Shaped Kitchen

An alternative to U Shaped, this layout enables openness and easy accessibility.

Ergonomics

The Kitchen is the most functional part of the house hence its design should minimise the movements and stress we go through while cooking. Ergonomics, which is the science of designing an environment for comfortable working, considers the heights of various surfaces, distances and placement of items.

SURFACE FINISHES

Laminate

It is a multi-layer synthetic wood product. It is created of high-density fibre, melamine resin or wood particles. They come with multiple options like suede, gloss, wooden and textured.

Membrane

It is basically a Polyvinyl chloride (PVC) foil which is wrapped around MDF (medium- density fibreboard) under high pressure. It is a good alternative to laminates and have similar options like wooden and gloss.

Lacquered Glass

Also known as "Back painted glass", this finish gives any shutters a premium look. Available in multiple solid colours, they are now proved to be one of the most popular finishes.

Acrylic

Made of polymers, this finish lends a glossy finish and has a seamless look. Very popular for kitchen shutters due to its reflective mirror- like finish and is a good alternative to lacquered glass.
