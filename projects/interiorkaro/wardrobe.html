Types of Wardrobes...

With the modular style, one has the liberty to design and align wardrobes as per available space and accordingly decide the number and kind of Shutters they want to go for.

Hinged Wardrobe

These are one of the most economical options. The doors are hinged to the carcass and swing open outwards giving complete visibility to the internal storage area.

Sliding Wardrobe

These are slightly costlier due to the sliding mechanism involved but are suitable for rooms with limited space. The sliding doors enable undisrupted access space but the visibility to the internal storage area is a bit compromised.

Floor to Ceiling

These are of a luxurious kind bringing grandeur by owning up the entire space from floor to ceiling. With the larger size, sliding doors and more vertical height, they have high storage efficiency.

Walk-in Closet

These are designed to convert a dedicated space to a wholesome experience where it's not only about organising and storing but also displaying and browsing one's belongings.

Cross Combination

Having a wardrobe shell with compartments and doors which could be a combination of sliding and hinged wardrobe. This style brings in flexibility to space and pricing.

Niche Wardrobe

Utilise a recessed area inside a room/ hallway to create a wardrobe by adding internal compartments and shutters without sacrificing additional square footage. This excludes the use of bottom/top/ side panels thereby reducing the cost.

S pace Organisation

A wardrobe should ideally match the personality of the user. The best part of a modular style wardrobe is that the segmentation of a single wardrobe can be done based on multiple personalities.

His Wardrobe

Wardrobe divided into compartments with ample space to facilitate hanging blazers, shirts and trousers. Additional drawers to store ties, socks, watches, etc.

Her Wardrobe

Wardrobe with large compartments to hang dresses, sarees, etc with shelves to store shoes and lockers for jewellery.

Kids Wardrobe

Wardrobes with open shelves and compartments at a lower height. Spaces more dedicated to store toys, books, bags, etc.

Ergonomics

With adequate planning and a basic understanding of the user, one can manipulate items and locations frequented in daily routine to make its accessibility safe and easy. Ergonomics helps to define such space with standard user-centric measurements.




Planning and Placing a Wardrobe...




  • Identify the most suitable space for a wardrobe.
  • Make sure the selected wall/corner is not prone to water spillage/seepage.
  • Avoid placing a wardrobe below an air conditioning unit.
  • Check for any L corners or a complete wall which can be used for a wardrobe.
  • Evaluate the space available in front of the wardrobe.
    - If there is less space, the most suitable would be Sliding/Floor to Ceiling shutters.
    - If there is space to club up a seat/dressing area, then one can opt for a walk-in style.
  • Plan the internals of the entire wardrobe/portion of it based on the user (he/she/kid).
  • The placement of a wardrobe is also dependent on or subject to alteration based on any possible obstruction in the space (column or beam).
  • Maximize the space by adding overhead lofts.
  • It's best not to compromise on the quality of the wardrobe. In case of a low budget, it is advisable to spend less on loose furniture/decor rather than opting for a cheaper material for the wardrobe.

SURFACE FINISHES

Laminate

It is a multi-layer synthetic wood product. It is created of high-density fibre, melamine resin or wood particles. They come with multiple options like suede, gloss, wooden and textured.

Membrane

It is basically a Polyvinyl chloride (PVC) foil which is wrapped around MDF (medium- density fibreboard) under high pressure. It is a good alternative to laminates and have similar options like wooden and gloss.

Lacquered Glass

Also known as "Back painted glass", this finish gives any shutters a premium look. Available in multiple solid colours, they are now proved to be one of the most popular finishes.

Acrylic

Made of polymers, this finish lends a glossy finish and has a seamless look. Very popular for kitchen shutters due to its reflective mirror- like finish and is a good alternative to lacquered glass.
